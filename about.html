My path to becoming a designer is unusual yet reasonable.

I once majored in math. The learning has trained my analytical skills. I always ask "why?". The process - to observe, to collect infos, to analyse, and to understand has been a natural tendency for me, which helps me a lot to understand humans and environments.
During my study in design, I tried design methods and process in different areas. And that enables me to specialize in putting ideas into practice no matter it’s digital or physical artefact.
With both analytical thinking and design thinking, I am able to work in a multidisciplinary team and to communicate with different kinds of people.
